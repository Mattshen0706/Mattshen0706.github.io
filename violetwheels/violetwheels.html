New York City thrives on its massive transportation network, moving millions across its boroughs daily. As part of PlaNYC’s push to improve quality of life and cut carbon emissions, the city has prioritized investments in public transit, walking, and biking. But despite these efforts, biking remains the least utilized option, with only 2.6% of commuters choosing it (PlaNYC). With 472 subway stations and over 3 million daily riders (MTA), the subway is the backbone of NYC’s commute. While it’s a step in the right direction for energy efficiency, there’s still huge potential for an even greener system, one that better integrates bikes and makes use of existing bike infrastructure. However, launching a citywide campaign to promote biking comes with challenges. Mayor Adams once explored subsidizing CitiBike in 2021, but budget constraints kept it from moving forward (Brachfeld). Rather than tackling NYC as a whole, a more practical starting point would be focusing on a specific community such as NYU. By working with NYU and CitiBike, we can work to introduce a discounted or complimentary CitiBike membership for students, making biking a more convenient and affordable option. Using NYU as a pilot program, we can collect data on how improved access impacts student ridership. If successful, this could serve as a model for expanding bike access across the city, making sustainable transportation a more realistic choice for more New Yorkers.
